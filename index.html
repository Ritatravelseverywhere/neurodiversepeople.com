What this assessment covers

Three presentations. One assessment.

The quiz adapts based on your early answers, routing you through the most relevant validated instruments for your profile.

⚡
ADHD

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder. Difficulty sustaining attention, impulsivity, restlessness, and executive function challenges — often misunderstood as laziness or carelessness.

🧩
Autism (ASC)

Autism Spectrum Condition. Differences in social communication, sensory processing, and a need for routine or predictability — frequently masked or undetected, especially in women.

✦
AuDHD

Co-occurring ADHD and autism. Increasingly recognised as a distinct presentation with unique challenges — where ADHD impulsivity and autistic need for routine directly conflict.

How it works

Adaptive, not one-size-fits-all

The quiz is structured in phases. Your early answers determine which follow-up assessments you receive — so you only answer what's relevant to you.

1
Initial screens

You complete a short ADHD screen (ASRS Part A, 6 questions) and autism screen (AQ-10, 10 questions).

2
Adaptive path

Based on your scores, the quiz routes you into ADHD, Autism, or AuDHD follow-up questions — skipping what's not relevant.

3
Deeper assessment

If autism traits are indicated, you're offered the RAADS-R — a comprehensive 40-question instrument across four dimensions.

4
Your NeuroProfile

You receive a visual profile with scores, clinical threshold comparisons, and personalised next-step recommendations.

Clinical instruments

Built on validated research tools

Every question in this assessment comes from a peer-reviewed, clinically validated instrument. These are the same tools used by NHS and private specialists.

ASRS-v1.1
Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale

Developed by the World Health Organisation. The most widely used adult ADHD screening tool globally, with strong published sensitivity and specificity.

AQ-10
Autism Spectrum Quotient (10-item)

Developed by Professor Simon Baron-Cohen at Cambridge University. A brief, validated autism screening tool recommended by NICE for adult referrals.

CAT-Q
Camouflaging Autistic Traits Questionnaire

Developed by Dr Laura Hull (UCL). Measures masking, compensation, and assimilation — crucial for identifying autistic adults who present as neurotypical.

RAADS-R
Ritvo Autism Asperger Diagnostic Scale

A comprehensive 40-item instrument covering social relatedness, language, sensory-motor, and circumscribed interests. Used in clinical assessment settings.
