Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026  ·  Applies to neurodiversepeople.com

Summary: The quiz itself collects no personal data and stores nothing on any server. We use Google AdSense (only with your consent) which sets advertising cookies. We use Amazon affiliate links which may set cookies on amazon.co.uk when you click them.

1. Who we are

NeuroDiverse People is operated as an independent website at neurodiversepeople.com. For the purposes of UK GDPR, the data controller is the site owner. For data-related enquiries, please contact us at: hello@neurodiversepeople.com

2. What data we collect

Quiz data

The screening quiz runs entirely in your browser. No quiz answers, scores, or results are ever sent to or stored on any server. All quiz data exists only in your browser's memory and is permanently deleted when you close or refresh the page.

Data collected automatically

When you visit this website, standard web server logs may record your IP address, browser type, and pages visited. This data is retained by GitHub (who host this site) in accordance with their Privacy Statement. We do not have direct access to these logs.

4. Google AdSense

With your consent, we display advertisements served by Google AdSense. Google uses cookies to show you relevant ads based on your browsing history. Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to this and other websites.

You can opt out of personalised advertising at any time via Google's ad settings or by visiting aboutads.info.

For more information on how Google uses data, see Google's Privacy Policy.

5. Amazon Associates affiliate links

This site participates in the Amazon Associates Programme, an affiliate advertising programme designed to provide a means for sites to earn advertising fees by linking to Amazon. When you click a book link on the results page, you are taken to Amazon's website and Amazon may set cookies on your device. We earn a small commission if you make a purchase, at no extra cost to you.

Our Amazon tracking tag is neurodiversep-21. For information on Amazon's data practices, see Amazon's Privacy Notice.

6. Your rights under UK GDPR

Under the UK General Data Protection Regulation, you have the following rights:

  • Right to access — request a copy of any personal data we hold about you
  • Right to erasure — request deletion of your personal data
  • Right to restrict processing — ask us to limit how we use your data
  • Right to object — object to our processing of your data
  • Right to withdraw consent — withdraw cookie consent at any time using the buttons above

As we collect very little personal data directly, most of these rights are most relevant in relation to our third-party providers (Google, Amazon). To exercise your rights or make a data request, contact us at hello@neurodiversepeople.com.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

7. Third-party links

This site contains links to external websites including Amazon and NHS resources. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and encourage you to read their privacy policies.

8. Children

This site is intended for adults aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 18.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The date at the top of this page will reflect when it was last updated. Continued use of the site after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
